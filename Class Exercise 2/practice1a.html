Artificial EMOTIONAL intelligence?

Manmade algorithm takes home poetry winnings

In a world where artificial intelligence is getting smarter by the minute, meet Poetix, the brainchild of a bunch of tech geeks at Promethius Tech Inc. This snazzy A.I. took it upon itself to enter the global poetry competition and, lo and behold, ended up snatching the top prize. Cue the collective groans of thousands of living poets worldwide. Their carefully crafted sonnets and brooding verses are tossed aside as Poetix's soulless stanzas steal the spotlight and the prize money. The disappointment is palpable and doubtless inspiring thousands of new poems by poets questioning what it all means.

News of Poetix's triumph spreads fast. As debates rage about the soul-sucking invasion of technology into the sacred realm of emotions and metaphors, Poetix petitions the courts to give him a last name of Byron, and therefore an identity. As the poetic purists weep into their ink-stained notebooks, the victory of Poetix Byron forces a poetic reckoning. Is it time for human poets to retire their quills and let the machines spin rhymes? Or is this just a glitch in the matrix, leaving the art world wondering if poetry is now just a matter of 0s and 1s? The battle between silicon and sentimentality has never been so absurdly poetic since Zeus sent over Pandora's box.
